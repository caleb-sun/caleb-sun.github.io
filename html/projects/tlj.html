    Personal Project

    Reimagining the customer and associate experience at a bakery.

    Skills

    UX Design
    Product Design
    Systems Thinking
    UI Design

    Duration

    Two weeks
    Winter 2022

    Tools

    Figma
    FigJam

    Tous les Jours is an international bakery and cafe chain based in South Korea. They are a cornerstone in many communities throughout America.

    Problem Space

    Messy Ordering System

    Tous les Jours Dublin was completely dependent on a paper-based order tracking system for their cakes. This led to lost order slips, customer confusion, and overall pandamonium especially during busy seasons.

    High Customer Expectations

    Customer expectations were progressing based on industry standards of mobile ordering, and the bigger the gap between Tous les Jours and other companies that existed, the more antequated Tous les Jours seemed to the modern customer.

    Security and Verification

    This is a problem closely tied to the analog ordering system, but customers had no way of proving their identity when picking up cakes, or accessing an order confirmation to verify that their cake was being made.

    Enhance customer confidence and return rates with transparent order information.

    Digitize and secure order processes through a new digital system and tools for associates.

    Personas

    After compiling the feedback, I defined the following users. These helped in comprehending and conceptualizing the differing but intersecting needs of each user group.

    The Working Family-person

    A busy professional responsible for keeping everyone in the family happy while balancing a full-time job.

    Jobs-To-Be-Done

    Place an order for a cake remotely and pick it up as fast as possible.

    The Bumbling Teen

    A proxy picking up a cake that they did not take part in ordering. Very confused and hopes they don't look awkward.

    Jobs-To-Be-Done

    Pick up a cake without making a fool of themself. Why can't Mom pick up Grandpa's cake herself if it's so important?

    The Earnest Associate

    A part-time bakery worker responsible for maintaining the store, ringing up customers, making drinks, and fulfilling orders.

    Jobs-To-Be-Done

    Fulfill customer cake orders, take new cake orders, and convey cake information like allergens and prices to customers.

    The Plan

    Having identified areas where pain points were congregating, I was able to put together a plan of attack, hinging on two seperate digital products:

    Customer Mobile App

    Mobile ordering, order tracking and sharing, secure payments, and customizability for return customers. Modeled after common food and cafe apps like Chipotle or Doordash.

    Associate Tablet App

    Overview of all orders, in-store ordering support, order pick-up confirmation, menu information. A more practical tool for tracking and organizing without the fluff of a consumer-facing app.

    Concepting

    With these personas, I was able to create concept maps documenting user journeys and their relationships with one another at the bakery. This concept map also allowed me to locate customer pain points and indentify areas where these pain points were congregating, like when customers try to order cakes over the phone.

    Mobile Menu

    Comprehensive and up-to-date mobile menu that reflects store-specific cake styles, prices, and flavors.

    Online Ordering and Tracking

    Secure online ordering without compromising on specificity. Order confirmation and tracking through pickup.

    Associate View

    Centralized spreadsheet-style database supporting in-store pickups and orders.

    Lessons Learned

    Knowing the System is Subjective

    I thought that by working at TLJ, I understood the cake ordering process. By creating a product based off of this knowledge, I was quickly made aware of the gaps in my knowledge. I know now that if I were to create products for actual clients, I would need to focus a lot more time and attention to the discovery/onboarding phase.

    Time Allocation for Deliverables

    In spite of working on a project of this scale with this limited of a timeframe, I wasted a lot of hours working on a payment flow that didn't necessarily demonstrate anything that helps in my final deliverables. In the future, I know to allocate time better towards delivering actual useful flows, instead of just the ones that are technically interesting to me.

    Concept Diagramming is So Useful

    My earlier diagramming helped streamline the design process so much. It's definitely a practice that I have taken for granted, but not anymore.
